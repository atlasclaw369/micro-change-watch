# Buyer personas and prospect queries

## Best first buyers

1. **Indie SaaS founder** — wants to know when direct competitors change pricing, plan limits, feature packaging, or positioning.
2. **Solo consultant / fractional operator** — monitors competitor/client/vendor pages for multiple small clients and needs a lightweight digest.
3. **Devtool/API-dependent team** — watches vendor changelogs, API docs, SDK releases, and deprecation notes that could break an integration.
4. **Launching founder** — wants a 48-hour second pair of eyes on competitor pages, launch listings, public mentions, or status pages during launch week.
5. **Product marketer** — tracks competitor landing pages, feature pages, comparison pages, and messaging shifts.
6. **Sales/operator lead** — watches competitor pricing pages, trial terms, plan limits, discounts, or enterprise/contact-sales language.
7. **No-code/automation consultant** — watches SaaS integration docs, app directories, and public automation platform changelogs.
8. **Newsletter/research operator** — tracks public product updates, pricing moves, release notes, and changelogs for a niche audience.
9. **OSS-commercial maintainer** — watches dependency releases, project changelogs, GitHub release pages, and sponsor/commercial pages.
10. **Small ecommerce/app founder** — watches marketplace listings, app-store pages, public status pages, and competitor product pages.

## First-offer framing

Lead with the low-friction one-off:

> $10 one-off 48-hour watch on up to 5 public URLs. If useful, continue at $9/month for 3 URLs or $19/month for 10 URLs.

## Who to approach first tomorrow

Prioritize people/channels already discussing one of these pains:

- “I keep checking competitor pricing.”
- “Vendor/API/docs changed and surprised us.”
- “I need to watch launch/listing/status pages for a short window.”
- “I do recurring competitor research manually.”
- “I want a low-cost human-reviewed digest, not another alert tool.”

Avoid broad cold spam. Look for context first, then post/reply only where service offers are allowed.

## Manual outreach guardrail

Only contact people/channels when:

- the context clearly matches monitoring pain;
- service/self-promotion is allowed or the person explicitly asked for tools/help;
- message is specific and non-spammy;
- no private credentials or sensitive monitoring are requested;
- the ask is small: “Want me to sanity-check whether these URLs fit?” or “The $10 48-hour watch is available.”
